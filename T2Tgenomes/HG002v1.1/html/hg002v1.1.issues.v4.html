Description

This track shows the locations of known and suspected accuracy issues in the HG002v1.1 genome assembly. This particular track shows version 4 of the ht002v1.1_issues BED file.

These issues will be updated as newly suspected errors are submitted to our issue tracking site on github.

Display Conventions and Configuration

The names of entries in this track include the description and genomic locations of the issues as they appear on the github site.

Methods

The "issues and excluded regions" file of suspect regions of the T2T-HG002v1.1 assembly contains 3,172 regions comprised of 38.8Mb of the assembly (including 31.1Mb of gap N's replacing copies of the rDNA repeat on nine acrocentric chromosomes and 2.5Mb of rDNA sequence flanking the gaps or on the completed paternal copy of chromosome 13).

99.35% of the diploid genome length is deemed free of any known errors. Excepting the rDNA bases described above, the remaining 5.3 Mb of low-confidence sequence (0.09% of the assembly) were flagged by either base-level or structural evaluation, erring on the side of caution to ensure the integrity of the benchmark.

Included in the bases flagged in this track are:

  • 183.1kb (293 regions) discovered using Merqury (Rhie 2020 PMID: 32928274) that were the result of merging the locations of 31-mers in the assembly that were not found in PacBio HiFi Revio SPRQ and Element UltraQ reads whenever they were within 5kb of each other.
  • 31.6kb (794 regions) discovered from parental 31-mers from Illumina reads for HG002's parents, HG003 and HG004. These represent potential phasing errors, but do not include regions in the immunoglobulin genes where VDJ recombination may have led to switched haplotypes in the B-cell from which the cell line was created.
  • 4.9Mb (237 regions) flagged by NucFlag run on both the latest PacBio and ONT reads with excessive secondary alleles present in the alignments of both technologies, indicative of a possible structural or consensus issue.
  • 486.6kb (1,893 regions) flagged by DeepTrio using Element reads; 82.kb (410 regions) flagged by DeepPolisher and DeepVariant (Mastoras 2025 PMID: 40389286, Poplin 2018 PMID: 30247488); 3.8kb (19 regions) flagged by Sniffles (Smolka 2024 PMID: 38168980); and 4.5kb (20 regions) contributed in GitHub issue submissions; and 44.7kb (3 regions) discovered by T2T-Polish (McCartney 2022, PMID: 35361931).
